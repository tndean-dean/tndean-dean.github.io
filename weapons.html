Weapons

Find the right weapon for your playstyle.

Weapons Guide

Choosing the right weapon is one of the most important decisions in Monster Hunter Wilds. Each weapon has a unique playstyle, strengths, and difficulty level. Take a look at the weapons below to get a general understanding of each one.

Weapon Info

Weapon Difficulty Playstyle
Great Sword
 Hard Slow attacks but very high damage
Long Sword
 Medium Balanced with fast combos and mobility
Sword & Shield
 Easy Fast, flexible, and beginner-friendly
Hammer
 Medium Focuses on stunning monsters with heavy hits
Bow
 Medium Ranged weapon with various ammo types for different effects
Dual Blades
 Hard Extremely fast attacks but low damage per hit
Insect Glaive
 Hard Versatile with aerial attacks and a helpful insect companion capable of inflicting status effects
Charge Blade
 Hard Complex weapon that can switch between sword and axe modes, with powerful charged attacks
Switch Axe
 Hard Transforms between axe and sword modes, offering a mix of mobility and power with unique attack patterns and devastating elemental damage
Lance
 Easy Defensive weapon with a long reach, ideal for players who prefer a more methodical, bulwark playstyle
Gunlance
Medium Combines a lance with explosive shelling attacks, allowing for both defensive play and powerful bursts of damage
Light Bowgun
 Medium Ranged weapon with high mobility and a variety of ammo types for different effects
Heavy Bowgun
 Medium Ranged weapon with high damage and a variety of ammo types, but lower mobility than the Light Bowgun
Hunting Horn
 Hard Support weapon that can play melodies to provide buffs to the hunter and their allies, while also dealing damage with powerful blunt attacks

How to Choose a Weapon

  • For New players: Start with weapons that don't have a steep learning curve, like the Lance, Light Bowgun, or Long Sword
  • For players seeking High damage numbers: Try out the Great Sword or Charge Blade
  • For players prioritizing Mobility: Choose weapons with high mobility moves like the Bow, Insect Glaive, or Sword and Shield
  • For players preferring Support style: Use flexible weapons and even try out the ultimate support weapon: the Hunting Horn

Don't worry about picking the “best” weapon. Try a few and choose the one that feels the most fun and comfortable to use for you.

Quick Notes

Every weapon in the game is viable, meaning you can complete hunts with any of them. The key is learning your weapon's moves and practicing against monsters.
